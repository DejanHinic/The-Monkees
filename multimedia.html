Welcome to Multimedia

Fifty two years ago four “pre-fab” actors launched the most misunderstood band in rock history. “The Monkees” debuted on NBC Sept. 12, 1966, building the prototypical “boy band” template in the process. That mold helped launch future, teen-friendly groups. None can measure up to The Monkees. Can we expect the Backstreet Boys or New Kids on the Block to keep crooning into their 70s? More importantly, do any of their songs resonate the way “Daydream Believer” or “Pleasant Valley Sunday” still do?

The Hits

“Daydream Believer” was the Monkees' last #1 single before they drifted apart.

Billboard has compiled the Monkees' top 10 biggest Hot 100 hits, which includes all six of their top 10 singles and the group's three No. 1s: "Last Train to Clarksville," "I'm a Believer" and "Daydream Believer."
